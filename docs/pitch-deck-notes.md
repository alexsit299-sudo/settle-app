# Settle — Shark Tank Pitch Notes

## The Format (5 minutes total)

### 1. The Hook (30 sec)
Make them feel the pain before you say a word about the product.

> "Last Friday, I sent a text to my group chat. Six words: 'Where should we eat tonight?' Two hours later — 47 messages, zero restaurants, and we ordered Uber Eats from the same place we always get. Sound familiar?"

Pause. Let them laugh or nod.

> "This happens to millions of people every single day. We built the app that just answers the question. I'm [Name], and this is Settle."

---

### 2. The Problem (45 sec)
Make it real with numbers.

- The average friend group makes 3-5 group decisions per week
- "Where should we eat" alone accounts for an estimated 30-45 minutes of wasted time per week per group
- This isn't a niche problem — it's universal, daily, and deeply annoying
- Existing tools (Yelp, Google Maps) tell you WHERE to eat — nobody tells you WHAT to choose

---

### 3. The Product (60 sec)
Demo the flow live on a phone or show a video.

> "Here's how Settle works. Someone starts a decision — 'dinner tonight, 7pm.' Everyone in the group gets a notification. They answer 3 questions in under 10 seconds. Settle reads everyone's inputs, cross-references location, ratings, and availability — and delivers one answer. Tonight you're going to Nobu. Table booked. Done."

Key beats to hit:
- Speed (10 seconds per person)
- Fairness (majority rules, transparent reasoning)
- Personality (it's funny — show a microcopy moment)
- Booking integration (money moment)

---

### 4. The Business Model (45 sec)

Three revenue streams, all proven in adjacent markets:

**Stream 1 — Reservation Commissions**
We take $2-5 per cover booked through Settle.
OpenTable does $500M+ annually on this exact model.
We capture the decision moment they never touched.

**Stream 2 — Featured Placement**
Restaurants pay to be recommended first.
Think Google Ads, but for your dinner plans.

**Stream 3 — Settle Premium**
$6.99/month for unlimited decisions, preference memory, priority booking, and activity/trip tabs unlocked.

---

### 5. The Market (30 sec)

- OpenTable: $500M+/year
- Yelp: $1.2B revenue, majority restaurant-driven
- Resy (acquired by AmEx): hundreds of millions
- Settle sits *upstream* of all of them — we own the decision, they own the booking

Total addressable market: anyone with a friend group and a phone. That's everyone.

## Likely Shark Questions & Answers

**"Why wouldn't OpenTable or Yelp just build this?"**
They've had years. They're focused on supply (restaurants), not demand (the decision). We own the moment before anyone opens Yelp.

**"What stops someone from just copying this?"**
The data moat. Once Settle knows your group's preferences and history, switching to a competitor means starting from zero. Preference data is the product.

**"How do you handle groups that never respond?"**
Settle has a timeout — if someone doesn't respond in X minutes, their vote is skipped. The group moves forward. No one person holds the group hostage.

**"What's your user acquisition strategy?"**
Organic virality — every time someone starts a decision, their entire group downloads the app. One user = 4-5 installs. CAC is structurally low.

---

## Tone Notes for the Pitch
- Be conversational, not corporate
- Let the humor land — Settle's personality is part of the product
- Show the phone demo — abstract pitches lose Sharks fast
- Know your numbers cold — be specific on commission rates and market comps
