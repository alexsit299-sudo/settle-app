## Slide 5 — More Than A Restaurant App

**Headline:** The app is the entry point. The data is the business.

**Visual:** Iceberg diagram — restaurant app above water, data platform below

**Above the waterline (what users see):**
- Restaurant matchmaking
- Group decision engine
- Social feed of settled decisions

**Below the waterline (what makes this a real business):**
- Behavioral decision-making data — how people make choices with others in real time
- Taste graph — stated vs revealed preferences across millions of decisions
- Social influence networks — not who you follow, but who changes your decisions
- Life stage signals — dining patterns that reveal relationship status, income, career stage

**The key line:**
"Google knows what you searched. Instagram knows what you looked at. Settle knows how you decide — with other people, under social pressure, in real time. That data category doesn't exist yet."

## Delivery Notes

**Tone:** Conversational. You're talking to people who know you. Don't go corporate.

**The moment that wins the room:** Slide 1. If they laugh or nod at the group chat problem, you have them. Everything after is just details.

**The question that will come up:** "What if Google builds this?" 
Answer: "Google builds tools, not experiences. They've never built a product with personality and social dynamics at its core. And by the time they'd ship, we have a year of behavioral data they can't buy."

**The other question:** "How do you make money if it's free?"
Answer: "Same way Instagram and TikTok do — the users are the product. Restaurants and brands pay to reach our user base and buy our behavioral data. We sit upstream of a trillion-dollar industry and we never charge the people who use us."

**Length:** 15-20 minutes presentation, 10-15 minutes questions. Never go over 30 minutes total.
